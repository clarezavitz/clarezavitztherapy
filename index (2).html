About Me

Therapy rooted in
compassion

Hi, I'm Clare — a Registered Social Worker with a Master of Social Work (MSW) degree, offering virtual individual therapy to adults across Ontario.

My approach is warm, collaborative, and grounded in the belief that healing happens when people feel truly seen and heard. I create a safe, non-judgmental space where you can explore your thoughts and emotions at your own pace.

I draw on evidence-based modalities including Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), and trauma-informed care, tailoring every session to your unique needs and goals.

Whether you're navigating a major life change, working through grief, or simply feeling stuck — I'm here to help you reconnect with yourself and move forward with clarity and confidence.

Non-judgmental space

Every part of you is welcome here. I offer a compassionate, confidential environment free of shame or expectation.

Collaborative process

You are the expert on your own life. We'll work together to set goals and find approaches that feel right for you.

Evidence-informed care

I integrate research-backed therapeutic approaches with genuine human connection for lasting, meaningful change.

Accessible & virtual

FAQ

Your questions, answered

How do virtual sessions work?
+

Sessions take place via a secure, PHIPA-compliant video platform. All you need is a private space, a device with a camera and microphone, and a stable internet connection. I'll send you a link before each session.

How long does therapy take?
+

It depends on your goals and what you're working through. Some people feel significantly better after a few months; others choose to continue longer. We'll check in regularly and adjust based on how things are going. You're never locked in.

Is everything I share confidential?
+

Yes. Confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy. What you share stays between us, with a few legal exceptions — such as if there's risk of harm to yourself or others, or if records are required by a court. I'll explain these clearly in our first session.

How do I know if we're a good fit?
+

That's exactly what the free 15-minute consultation is for. There's no pressure and no commitment — it's simply a chance for us to connect, for you to ask questions, and for both of us to see if working together feels right.

What's the difference between a social worker and a psychologist?
+

Both can provide therapy, but the training paths differ. As a Registered Social Worker with an MSW, I'm trained in psychotherapy, mental health, and the broader social and environmental factors that influence wellbeing. RSW services are often covered by extended health benefits — check your plan under "Social Worker" or "RSW."
